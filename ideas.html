Personal Values

Awareness

"Self-awareness allows one to discover the capacity to persevere through challenges. Lack thereof causes suffering."

Authenticity

"When authentic, one is capable of reaching out to others and making true connection. Authenticy is born of awareness, for when one realizes that most habituated defenses are unwarranted for any given situation, one can relax into being authentic. Constricted awareness causes rigid, repetitive, behaviors and attitudes that do not express the expansiveness of the heart."

Love

"Caring and affection are perhaps the most fulfilling emotions. Through caring one learns to expand into something more than oneself."

Healing

"Processing stress enables one to think with clarity, unpolarized by reactivity."

Seeking Truth

"Seeking truth requires independence of thought. To become an independent thinker, one needs to learn to trust in one's ability to make wise decisions. Financial independence is also valuable in allowing one the economic freedom to think independently."

Family

"One of the strongest ways that love is expressed is though family bonds. The care that one receives and gives in a family context can drive one's growth as a person."


Metaphysical Beliefs

Interconnectedness

"I believe that there is a fundamental interconnectedness between all; nothing exists in absolute isolation from the whole (Spinoza). This interconnectedness allows for the possibility of events influencing on other events, even when no concrete connection between them is perceived."

Events

"I like Alfred North Whitehead's "Process Philosophy" that states that there are only events; there is no concrete material. What appears to be material are highly patterned events that have become so cyclical that they appear concrete."

Mentation

"I like Alfred North Whiteheads's theory of mind and body that states that mentation is not categorically distinct from the physical (contra Descarte's dichotomy). Both mentation and the physical are events of an organic universe (contra contemporary physicalists) whose only constant factors are creativity and entropy (Rupert Sheldrake, "Science Set Free", base on A.N. Whitehead, above)."

Decisions

"I believe that every decision sets a force toward its actuation into motion, and influences the decider's psychic constitution, and the psychic constitution of the decider's direct descendents. Thus, through decisions, one contributes to the development of one's evolutionary trajectory."
